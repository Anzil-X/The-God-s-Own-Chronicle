Historical Landmarks

Bekal Fort

Bekal Fort is one of the largest and most well-preserved forts in Kerala, situated on a beautiful beach overlooking the Arabian Sea. Built in the 17th century by Shivappa Nayaka of the Keladi dynasty, it was strategically located to protect the region from maritime invasions. The fort's imposing structure features a circular layout, with a wide moat and massive stone walls that reflect the military architecture of its time. Today, Bekal Fort is not only a historical monument but also a popular tourist destination, offering stunning views and insights into Kerala's rich history.
Location: Bekal, Kerala, India

Edakkal Caves

The Edakkal Caves are famous for their prehistoric petroglyphs and ancient carvings, believed to date back over 6,000 years. Discovered in 1895 by a British surveyor, these caves are home to some of the earliest human representations in India. Located on Ambukuthi Hill, the caves feature intricate rock art that provides valuable insights into the lives and beliefs of ancient civilizations. The trek to the caves is equally rewarding, offering panoramic views of Wayanad's lush landscapes. The Edakkal Caves serve as a testament to Kerala's rich archaeological heritage and are a significant attraction for history enthusiasts.
Location: Edakkal, Kerala, India.
Address: Edakkal Caves, Wayanad, Kerala 673591. View on Google Maps

Padmanabhapuram Palace

Padmanabhapuram Palace is a magnificent wooden palace that exemplifies the traditional Kerala architecture and royal heritage. Built in the 16th century as the residence of the Travancore royal family, the palace features intricate wood carvings, beautifully painted ceilings, and exquisite murals depicting Hindu mythology. The palace served as the administrative headquarters of the erstwhile princely state of Travancore and is known for its remarkable construction techniques using interlocking wooden joints without the use of nails. Today, it stands as a symbol of Kerala's rich cultural history and attracts visitors who seek to understand the region's royal past.
Location: Padmanabhapuram, Kerala, India.
Address: Padmanabhapuram Palace, Padmanabhapuram, Kerala 629177. View on Google Maps
